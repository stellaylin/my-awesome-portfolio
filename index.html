Officer Biography

This is a piece I designed to introduce executive members of one of my student organizations, Undergraduate Trading Society. The executive officers of this professional organization often come off as intimidating and difficult to talk to for general members, so I wanted to bring a more lighthearted vibe while still keeping the executive members professional. As such, I used their professional photos overlaid onto a graph paper style background. The brush strokes further contributed to a more "rough-hewn" look, while the fonts were more bubbly without being too informal.

Event Schedule Flyer

A flyer I designed to summarize the quarterly events hosted by my student organization. Because we are a cultural organization, my goal was to show as much cultural connection as possible, while keeping the design simple, modern, and easy to read. I utilized pagodas for my background, a shot taken in Kaohsiung, a city in South Taiwan. I truly believe this flyer increased event turnout, as people were able to mark dates on their calendars. The flyer is also quite memorable, as most of our members have a similar cultural background. The background photo may have resonated near and dear to their hearts.

Facebook Event Banner

Every year, my student organization hosts a banquet to recap the successful year we had. Unfortunately, the banquet was forced to a virtual event. What used to be an exciting, somewhat formal event with good food and conversation turned into an another awkward Zoom meeting. My board and I did the best that we could to mitigate feelings of boredom during the virtual event. As such, the theme we chose was Animal Crossing: New Horizons, as it was very popular around the time we hosted our event. The event turned out to be a smashing success, with many people getting excited over the Animal Crossing theme.

Facebook Event Banner for Hikeathlon

A piece I designed for a hikeathlon, de-stress style event put on by one of my student organizations. The concept behind this piece was to embody the crisp, carefree nature of the hike. The goal was to attract stressed students. Though simple, this is one of my favorite designs!

Fundraiser Banner

This piece is a Facebook banner I curated to promote a fundraiser my student organization, Taiwanese American Student Association, was hosting. I stuck with a cute and handdrawn concept for this banner to really highlight the food we were selling. Using sketch-style artwork worked out for the better, as a real picture of meat is not always appetizing.

ATLA: Water Tribe Inspired Nail Art

Beauty is something I have been passionate about since I was a teenager. I started doing nail art since I was in middle school, at first just experimenting with different tools and colors on my own fingertips. In high school, my designs got more and more elaborate. I received compliments on my nails from the lunch ladies and the librarians. They were such a great topipc of conversation, I even wrote about them in one of my college application essays. During quarantine, I've wanted to expand my hobby into something more. I'm currently working on launching luxury press-on nails that feature intricate designs.
